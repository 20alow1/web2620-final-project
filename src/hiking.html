Hiking

“There are no shortcuts to any place worth going”

~ Beverly Sills

Hiking is one of my favorite things to do, and I've been doing it with my family and friends ever since I was little. I've been to a lot of cool places, but I chose some of the more recent ones I've hiked to display here. Most of them are pretty close to where I live, but one of them is in another state! I enjoy hiking a lot, and I want to be able to enjoy nature while I am still young because it is so beautiful!

Grinnell Glacier

This hike is in Glacier National Park, Montana, which is definitely worth visiting if you haven't been there before! The ice-cold lake and glacier are naturally the main attraction of this hike, but the scenery along the way was amazing too, with lots of tall mountains and shimmering lakes at their feet…

Skull Crack

This trail takes you in a curve along a river, high above the shoreline. Depending on the time of year you go, there are lots of wildflowers speckled here and there as you hike along the trail. There’s not really an end destination, but if you want a nice scenery walk, this is a good one.

Mt. Timpanogos

You can start this hike in a few different places, but the place I chose to start the hike was at the Timpooneke trail, which is close to the Timpooneke campground. It was definitely a challenging hike, but the view, the feeling of accomplishment, and writing your name in the book at the top all make it worth the time.

Lake Blanche

Personally, I would say this hike is better to do in the fall or winter when there is snow in the mountains, because it accents the landscape very nicely, especially with the reddish-orange rock that doubles as a nice place to sit. I’m sure it would be beautiful any time of the year you decide to go, of course.

Ogden Canyon Overlook

This trail starts at the Snowbasin Ski Resort and takes you to the top of a mountain where you can see the city of Ogden framed between two mountains. Along the way, there are also several points on the trail where you can look back and see the resort and the landscape around it.
